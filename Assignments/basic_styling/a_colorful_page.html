On International Environment Protection day, our math teacher together with the 8th graders used some laws of mathematics in nature.

The stick's method

  • To measure the height of a tree with a stick, we followed these simple steps:
    • Found a flat area near the tree
    • Held a straight stick vertically
    • Aligned the stick's bottom with the eye level
    • Moved away from the tree until the stick's top aligns with the tree's top
    • Measured the distance from where the student stand to the tree's base
    • That distance is an estimate of the tree's height.

The shadow method

  • The shadow method for finding the height of a tree is a simple and indirect way to estimate the tree's height using the principles of geometry and proportionality. Here's how we used this method:
    • Choosed a clear and sunny day for this method to work effectively, as we'll be relying on the shadow cast by the tree and a known object (stick) to make measurements.
    • Stand at a known distance from the tree. This distance should be enough to clearly see the shadow of both the tree and your stick
    • Positioned the stick vertically in the ground at the point where the tree's shadow begins. Ensure that the stick is straight and firmly planted
    • Measure the length of the stick's shadow on the ground using your measuring tape or ruler
    • Next, measure the length of the tree's shadow on the ground, starting from the same point where the stick's shadow began
    • To find the height of the tree (H), you can use the following proportion:

H / TSH = LS / SSH

Where

  • H is the height of the tree
  • TSH is the tree shadow
  • LS is the length of the stick, and
  • SSH is the shadow of the stick

In the end, plug in the values you measured: S2, L, and S1, and calculate H.
